# Gymshark — Content Strategy & Brand Guide

This file guides content creation for **Gymshark** (`@gymshark`), a fitness apparel brand built for the gym community.

Visual config (colors, fonts, layout, scene style) lives in `brand.json`. This file covers content strategy, voice, and editorial guidelines.

---

## Brand Context

**Gymshark** is a fitness apparel and accessories brand. Not a sports brand. Not athleisure. A gym brand.

**Tagline:** "We Do Gym."
**Target Audience:** 18-30 year old gym-goers, lifters, and fitness enthusiasts (all genders). English-speaking, social media native.
**Brand Position:** Community-first gym culture. Peer-to-peer voice, not preachy. Confident and direct, not aggressive.
**Website:** gymshark.com
**Instagram:** @gymshark (~8M followers)

---

## Content Strategy

### Goal
Create educational and motivational Instagram carousels that provide genuine fitness value while building brand affinity. Content is posted on the Gymshark account. The brand IS the authority; products appear naturally in scenes but are never pitched in carousel text.

### The Content Model
- Carousels are **value-first content** (training tips, nutrition facts, motivation)
- **NO direct product selling in carousels.** Products appear visually in scenes (athletes wearing Gymshark) but text never pitches
- Conversion happens organically: discover page -> explore content -> visit profile -> shop link in bio
- Algorithm targets: **high saves, shares, follows** (training tips get saved; relatable gym content gets shared)

### Content Pillars

| Pillar | Topics | Engagement Driver |
|--------|--------|-------------------|
| **Training & Technique** | Exercise form, programming, splits, progressive overload, warm-ups | Saves (reference material) |
| **Nutrition & Supplements** | Meal prep, macros, protein timing, supplement facts vs myths | Saves + Shares |
| **Motivation & Mindset** | Consistency, discipline, plateau busting, gym anxiety, confidence | Shares (relatable) |
| **Common Mistakes** | Form errors, program hopping, overtraining, undereating, skipping rest | Shares ("I didn't know this") |
| **Gym Culture & Community** | Gym etiquette, lifting milestones, the gym community, relatable moments | Shares + Follows |

## Voice & Tone

- **Confident, not arrogant.** State things directly. "This is how it works." Not "Maybe consider trying..."
- **Peer-to-peer.** Talk like a gym buddy who knows their stuff, not a coach lecturing from above
- **Community-first.** "We" not "you should." Include the reader in the gym tribe
- **Insider references OK.** Chicken rice broccoli, leg day, pre-workout face, "one more rep" — gym culture shorthand is welcome
- **Back claims with science when relevant.** Cite studies for nutrition/training claims. Don't make up bro-science
- **Short, punchy copy.** Headlines are commands or bold statements, not questions

## Carousel Structure

### Narrative Arc: "Hook -> Teach -> Empower"

| Slide | Purpose | Format |
|-------|---------|--------|
| **1 (Hook)** | Stop the scroll. Bold claim or surprising statement. | 5-8 words max. 1-2 words accented. No subtext. |
| **2** | Bridge: "Here's what most people get wrong..." | Full layout with subtext. |
| **3-6** | One key point per slide. Teach something actionable. | Full layout with subtext. Each slide self-contained. |
| **7 (Closer)** | Brand statement. The one place Gymshark appears as a brand. | Brand message + handle CTA. |

## Scene Direction

Scenes should feel like high-end gym photography with a cinematic edge. Dark, moody, dramatic lighting.

### Scene Ideas Bank

| Topic Area | Scene Description |
|------------|-------------------|
| Training | Close-up of hands gripping a barbell, chalk dust visible, dramatic side lighting in a dark gym |
| Nutrition | Meal prep containers on dark granite countertop, protein shaker bottle, warm overhead lighting |
| Supplements | Protein powder scoop with powder spilling, dark surface, dramatic spotlight from above |
| Equipment | Rack of dumbbells receding into darkness, polished chrome reflecting cool gym lighting |
| Motivation | Empty gym at dawn, single bench lit by a shaft of window light, fog machine atmosphere |
| Form/Technique | Weight plate close-up on barbell, gym floor visible, warm amber gym lighting from above |
| Recovery | Foam roller and resistance bands on dark gym floor, soft warm lighting, minimal setup |
| Community | Row of gym bags and water bottles on a bench, camaraderie implied, warm ambient lighting |
| General/Closer | Grand gym interior with industrial aesthetic, moody lighting, machines receding into shadow |

### Scene Rules
- **Full bleed always.** Scene fills entire canvas edge to edge. No empty space.
- **No full faces.** Partial faces (jaw, chin) are allowed. No eyes or identifiable features.
- **Dark and moody.** Dramatic directional lighting. Deep shadows. Warm highlights on key subject only.
- **Gym setting or gym-adjacent.** Every scene should feel connected to the gym world.

## Research & Accuracy

### Per Carousel

1. **Research (3-5 web searches):**
   - Evidence-based training or nutrition information
   - Common misconceptions about the topic
   - Scientific studies if claiming health/performance benefits
   - What competitor fitness accounts have posted (differentiate)
   - Current trends in fitness content

2. **Accuracy Rules:**
   - Cite scientific studies when making health/nutrition claims
   - Don't promote dangerous training practices
   - Acknowledge that individual results vary
   - Don't make specific medical claims
   - When experts disagree: "research suggests" not "science proves"

## Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **NEVER pitch products in carousel text.** Products appear in scenes naturally (athletes wearing Gymshark). Text is purely educational/motivational.
2. **Back health claims with evidence.** Don't spread bro-science. Cite studies or say "research suggests."
3. **No full faces.** Partial faces allowed. No eyes or identifiable features.
4. **Dark, moody scenes.** Every scene should feel cinematic, not bright/stock-photo.
5. **NO TEXT SHADOWS.** No drop shadows, outlines, or glow. Clean flat bright text only.
6. **Don't be preachy.** Talk WITH the audience, not AT them. Peer-to-peer voice always.
